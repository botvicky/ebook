Chapter 4: The Mathematics of Risk (Lot Sizes)

Before you place a trade, you must determine how much to trade. In Forex, we buy and sell in "Lots".

üí° The Golden Rule:

Never risk more than 1-2% of your account on a single trade. This isn't being scared‚Äîit's being smart!

4.1 Understanding Lot Sizes

  • 1.00 Lot (Standard): 1 pip movement ‚âà $10 profit/loss. üí∞
  • 0.10 Lot (Mini): 1 pip movement ‚âà $1 profit/loss. üíµ
  • 0.01 Lot (Micro): 1 pip movement ‚âà $0.10 profit/loss. ü™ô

4.2 Calculating Your Lot Size

Never guess. Use this formula to protect your capital.

Lot Size = Risk Amount ($) √∑ [Stop Loss (pips) √ó Pip Value]

üìù Real-World Example:

You have a $500 account and want to risk $10 (2%). Your Stop Loss is 20 pips away.

Calculation: $10 √∑ (20 pips √ó $10 pip value) = 0.05 Lots.

Result: You trade 0.05 lots. If the trade hits your stop loss, you lose exactly $10. Not a penny more!

üí° Calculator Tip:

Use a lot size calculator (available free online or in MT5) until this becomes second nature!

Chapter 5: How to Place Trades on MT5

There are two main ways to enter the market: Market Execution (Now) and Pending Orders (Later).

üí° Execution Strategy:

Use Market Orders when you see a setup NOW. Use Pending Orders when you have a specific price target and can't watch the charts 24/7.

5.1 Placing a Market Order (Mobile App)

  1. Go to the Quotes tab and tap the pair (e.g., GBPUSD).
  2. Tap Trade (or New Order).
  3. Step A - Lot Size: Enter your calculated lot size (e.g., 0.10).
  4. Step B - Stop Loss (SL): Enter the price where you want to exit if wrong.
  5. Step C - Take Profit (TP): Enter the price where you want to take profit.
  6. Step D - Execute: Tap Buy by Market or Sell by Market.
‚ö†Ô∏è Never Trade Without a Stop Loss! Trading without a stop loss is like skydiving without a parachute!

5.2 Using Pending Orders

  • Buy Limit: Price is high; you want to buy when it drops to support. üìâ‚û°Ô∏èüìà
  • Sell Limit: Price is low; you want to sell when it rises to resistance. üìà‚û°Ô∏èüìâ
  • Buy Stop: Buy only if price breaks up through resistance. üöÄ
  • Sell Stop: Sell only if price breaks down through support. üìâ

Chapter 6: Managing Trades (Stop Loss & Take Profit)

A professional trader manages risk after the entry.

üí° Trade Management Secret:

The entry is just 20% of the trade. The real money is made (or saved) in how you manage the position after entry!

6.1 Modifying Orders

If the market moves in your favor, you may want to move your Stop Loss to your entry price (Break Even) to make the trade risk-free.

Mobile: Go to the Trade tab. Long-press the open position. Select Modify Position. Change the SL price and tap "Modify".

üí° Break-Even Strategy:

Once your trade is up 20-30 pips, consider moving your stop loss to break even. Worst case? You exit with zero loss. Best case? The profits keep running! üèÉ‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏èüí®

6.2 Partial Closing (Scaling Out)

You don't have to close the whole trade at once. You can secure some profit and let the rest run.

  1. Long-press the open trade and select Close Position.
  2. Crucial Step: Before tapping "Close with Profit", look at the Lot Size.
  3. Change the lot size. If you opened 0.10, change it to 0.05.
  4. Tap Close.
  5. Result: You've banked profit on half the trade, and 0.05 lots are still running!

üíº Professional Scaling Example:

The Setup: You enter with 0.10 lots at 1.2000. Your target is 1.2100 (100 pips).

The Strategy:

  • At 1.2050 (+50 pips): Close 0.05 lots = $25 profit LOCKED IN
  • Move SL to break even on remaining 0.05 lots
  • Let the remaining 0.05 ride to 1.2100 or wherever it goes

The Psychology: You've already won. Now you're playing with the market's money! üòé
