Chapter 4: The Mathematics of Risk (Lot Sizes)

Before you place a trade, you must determine how much to trade. In Forex, we buy and sell in "Lots".

üí° The Golden Rule:

Never risk more than 1-2% of your account on a single trade. This isn't being scared‚Äîit's being smart!

4.1 Understanding Lot Sizes

Lot Type Units Example (EUR/USD)
Standard Lot 100,000 units 1.00
Mini Lot 10,000 units 0.10
Micro Lot 1,000 units 0.01

4.2 Calculating Your Position Size

Formula: Lot Size = (Account Balance √ó Risk %) √∑ (Stop Loss in Pips √ó Pip Value)

Example:
  • Account Balance: $1,000
  • Risk: 1% = $10
  • Stop Loss: 50 pips
  • Pip Value (for 0.01 lot): $0.10

Calculation: $10 √∑ (50 √ó $0.10) = 2 micro lots (0.02)

Chapter 5: How to Place Trades on MT5

There are two main ways to enter the market: Market Execution (Now) and Pending Orders (Later).

üí° Execution Strategy:

Use Market Orders when you see a setup NOW. Use Pending Orders when you have a specific price target and can't watch the charts 24/7.

5.1 Market Execution (Instant Entry)

  1. Right-click on a chart ‚Üí Trading ‚Üí New Order
  2. Type: Select "Market Execution"
  3. Volume: Enter your lot size (e.g., 0.01 for micro lot)
  4. Stop Loss & Take Profit: Set your exit levels
  5. Click Buy or Sell

5.2 Pending Orders (Set & Forget)

Types of pending orders:

  • Buy Limit: Buy when price drops to your target
  • Sell Limit: Sell when price rises to your target
  • Buy Stop: Buy when price breaks above resistance
  • Sell Stop: Sell when price breaks below support
‚ö†Ô∏è Execution Alert: Always double-check your lot size before clicking Buy/Sell. One extra zero can wipe out your account!

Chapter 6: Managing Trades (Stop Loss & Take Profit)

A professional trader manages risk after the entry.

üí° Trade Management Secret:

The entry is just 20% of the trade. The real money is made (or saved) in how you manage the position after entry!

6.1 Stop Loss (SL) ‚Äì Your Safety Net

A Stop Loss is a pre-set exit point that closes your trade automatically if the market moves against you.

‚ö†Ô∏è Non-Negotiable Rule: NEVER trade without a Stop Loss. Ever. No exceptions. Not even "just this once."

How to Set a Stop Loss:

  1. Identify support/resistance levels
  2. Place SL 5-10 pips beyond these levels
  3. Ensure the SL respects your 1-2% risk rule

6.2 Take Profit (TP) ‚Äì Lock in Gains

A Take Profit automatically closes your trade when it reaches your profit target.

TP Strategy: Use a minimum 1:2 risk-reward ratio. If you risk 50 pips, aim for 100+ pips profit.

Pro Move: Trailing Stop

As your trade moves into profit, move your SL to break-even, then trail it behind price. This locks in profits while giving the trade room to run!
