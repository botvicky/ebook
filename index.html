Chapter 4: The Mathematics of Risk (Lot Sizes)

Before you place a trade, you must determine how much to trade. In Forex, we buy and sell in "Lots".

üí° The Golden Rule of Lot Sizing:

Never risk more than 1-2% of your account on a single trade. This isn't being scared‚Äîit's being smart. Even the best traders lose sometimes. The goal is to survive to trade another day!

4.1 Understanding Lot Sizes

  • 1.00 Lot (Standard): 1 pip movement ‚âà $10 profit/loss. üí∞
  • 0.10 Lot (Mini): 1 pip movement ‚âà $1 profit/loss. üíµ
  • 0.01 Lot (Micro): 1 pip movement ‚âà $0.10 profit/loss. ü™ô

4.2 Calculating Your Lot Size

Never guess. Use this formula to protect your capital.

Lot Size = Risk Amount ($) √∑ [Stop Loss (pips) √ó Pip Value]

üìù Real-World Example:

You have a $500 account and want to risk $10 (2%). Your Stop Loss is 20 pips away.

Calculation: $10 √∑ (20 pips √ó $10 pip value) = 0.05 Lots.

Result: You trade 0.05 lots. If the trade hits your stop loss, you lose exactly $10. Not a penny more!

üí° Calculator Tip:

Use a lot size calculator (available free online or in MT5) until this becomes second nature. Accurate lot sizing is the difference between growing your account and blowing it up!

Chapter 5: How to Place Trades on MT5

There are two main ways to enter the market: Market Execution (Now) and Pending Orders (Later).

üí° Execution Strategy:

Use Market Orders when you see a setup NOW. Use Pending Orders when you have a specific price target and can't watch the charts 24/7. Smart traders use both!

5.1 Placing a Market Order (Mobile App)

  1. Go to the Quotes tab and tap the pair (e.g., GBPUSD).
  2. Tap Trade (or New Order).
  3. Step A - Lot Size: Enter your calculated lot size in the top center (e.g., 0.10).
  4. Step B - Stop Loss (SL): In the field with the Red line, enter the price where you want to exit if you are wrong.
  5. Step C - Take Profit (TP): In the field with the Green line, enter the price where you want to take profit.
  6. Step D - Execute: Tap the big Buy by Market or Sell by Market button.
‚ö†Ô∏è Never Trade Without a Stop Loss! Trading without a stop loss is like skydiving without a parachute. Sure, it feels free... until it doesn't!

5.2 Using Pending Orders

Use these when you want to enter at a specific price, not the current price.

  • Buy Limit: Price is currently high; you want to buy when it drops to a lower support level. üìâ‚û°Ô∏èüìà
  • Sell Limit: Price is currently low; you want to sell when it rises to a resistance level. üìà‚û°Ô∏èüìâ
  • Buy Stop: You want to buy only if the price breaks up through a resistance level. üöÄ
  • Sell Stop: You want to sell only if the price breaks down through a support level. üìâ
üéØ How to set: In the order window, tap "Market Execution" and change it to the pending order type. Enter the Price where you want the order to trigger, then set your SL and TP as normal.

Chapter 6: Managing Trades (Stop Loss & Take Profit)

A professional trader manages risk after the entry.

üí° Trade Management Secret:

The entry is just 20% of the trade. The real money is made (or saved) in how you manage the position after entry. Never "set and forget" completely!

6.1 Modifying Orders

If the market moves in your favor, you may want to move your Stop Loss to your entry price (Break Even) to make the trade risk-free.

Mobile: Go to the Trade tab. Long-press the open position. Select Modify Position. Change the SL price and tap "Modify".

üí° Break-Even Strategy:

Once your trade is up 20-30 pips, consider moving your stop loss to break even. This way, worst case scenario, you exit with zero loss. Best case? The profits keep running! üèÉ‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏èüí®

6.2 Partial Closing (Scaling Out)

You don't have to close the whole trade at once. You can secure some profit and let the rest run.

  1. Long-press the open trade and select Close Position.
  2. Crucial Step: Before you tap the orange "Close with Profit" button, look at the Lot Size at the top.
  3. Change the lot size. If you opened 0.10, change it to 0.05.
  4. Tap Close.
  5. Result: You have banked profit on half the trade, and 0.05 lots are still running in the market.

üíº Professional Scaling Example:

The Setup: You enter with 0.10 lots at 1.2000. Your target is 1.2100 (100 pips).

The Strategy:

  • At 1.2050 (+50 pips): Close 0.05 lots = $25 profit LOCKED IN
  • Move SL to break even on remaining 0.05 lots
  • Let the remaining 0.05 ride to 1.2100 or wherever it goes

The Psychology: You've already won. Now you're playing with the market's money! üòé
